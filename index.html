Edie Windsor

“I didn’t want to live a life without love.”

Timeline

  • 1929 - Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • 1950 - Earned bachelor's degree from Temple University
  • 1955 - Began pursuing a master's degree in Mathematics at New York University
  • 1957 - Obtained master degree and then joined IBM
  • 1958 - Began technical and management positions at IBM
  • 1963 - Windsor met Thea Spyer, an Amsterdam-born psychologist
  • 1965 - Windsor began dating Spyer and kept a secret from her coworkers
  • 1967 - Spyer asked Windsor to marry
  • 1968 - Attained the title designating the highest level technical position at IBM, Senior Systems Programmer. However, IBM rejected her insurance form naming her partner Thea Spyer as a beneficiary
  • 1975 - IBM moved Windsor's group out of the area, and she took a severance package to focus more on her activism
  • 1977 - Spyer was diagnosed with progressive multiple sclerosis, and Windsor used her early retirement to become a full-time caregiver for Spyer
  • 1993 - Windsor and Spyer entered a domestic partnership in New York City
  • 2007 - New York has not yet legalized same-sex marriage, so the couple opted to marry in Toronto, Canada
  • 2009 - Spyer died from complications related to her heart condition. Windsor was required to pay $363,053 in federal estate taxes on her inheritance of her wide's estate. Had federal law recognized the validity of their marriage, Windsor would have qualified for an unlimited spousal deduction and paid no federal estate taxes
  • 2010 - Windsor filed a lawsuit against the federal government in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, seeking a refund because DOMA singled out legally married same-sex couples for differential treatment compared to other similarly situated couples without justification
  • 2012 - Judge Barbara S. Jones ruled that Section 3 of DOMA was unconstitutional
  • 2013 - The U.S. Supreme Court heard oral arguments in March, and in June issued a 5-4 decision affirming that Section 3 of DOMA was unconstitutional "as a deprivation of the liberty of the person protected by the Fifth Amendment"
  • 2016 - Lesbians Who Tech initiated the Edie Windsor Coding Scholarship Fund
  • 2017 - Dies at the age of 88
